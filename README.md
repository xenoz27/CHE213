# CHE213 — Upward Single Drop Mass Transfer in Water

CHE213: Mass Transfer — Unit Operations Lab, Group 10

This repository contains the final report and presentation for a lab study investigating mass transfer during the upward motion of a single liquid drop rising through a continuous water phase.

## Overview

Liquid-liquid mass transfer plays a central role in extraction and separation processes across chemical engineering. This project studies the mass transfer behavior of a single drop as it rises through a stagnant water column, focusing on how drop dynamics (rise velocity, drop size, and contact time) influence the rate of mass transfer between the dispersed and continuous phases.

The study examines:

- The motion and deformation of a single drop as it rises through water.
- The estimation of mass transfer coefficients based on drop rise time and concentration change.
- Comparison of experimental observations against theoretical/empirical mass transfer correlations for single drops.
